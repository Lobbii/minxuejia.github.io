About me

I am a first-year Postdoctoral Associate in the Division of Rheumatology and Clinical Immunology at the University of Pittsburgh, working under the mentorship of Dr. Robert Lafyatis. I earned my Ph.D. in Computational Biology through the Joint Carnegie Mellon University–University of Pittsburgh (CPCB) Program in 2025, under the supervision of Dr. Panayiotis (Takis) Benos. I also hold a Master of Science in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering from Johns Hopkins University and a B.S. with a double major in Chemical Engineering and Chemistry from Worcester Polytechnic Institute.

My research interests lie at the interface of machine learning and biology. I develop and apply causal discovery and deep learning methods to identify risk factors and mechanisms underlying aging and the onset and progression of chronic disease.
